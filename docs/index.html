Universal macOS binary · Intel + Apple Silicon · macOS 11+

Your journal stays on your Mac

No accounts. No cloud. A kill-switch in the main process blocks every outbound request by default — your decisions never leave your machine.

Encrypted at rest

A 256-bit key encrypts your database. Your PIN wraps that key via Argon2id, and the macOS keychain wraps it again. Copy the file off your disk and it looks like random noise.

Local AI, on your machine

Chat with a local Ollama model about past decisions. Dictate new ones with on-device Whisper. No prompts, no audio, and no text ever leaves your Mac.

Inspired by Farnam Street's decision journal practice — write down a decision as you're making it, come back to it months later, log how it actually played out, and slowly build a real picture of your own judgment over time.
